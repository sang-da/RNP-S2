# 07 - Pedagogical Frameworks and Theories

To establish the RNP Manager as a serious educational tool suitable for a Zenodo publication, its mechanics are grounded in well-established pedagogical and psychological theories.

## 1. Self-Determination Theory (SDT)
Developed by Deci & Ryan, SDT states that intrinsic motivation is fueled by three basic psychological needs:
*   **Autonomy**: RNP Manager removes rigid assignments. Agencies determine *how* they will earn PiXi, *who* they will hire from the Mercato, and *what* strategy they will adopt (e.g., specializing in high-risk "Black Ops" or steady, foundational missions).
*   **Competence**: The real-time leaderboard (VE) and the individual bank accounts offer immediate, visible feedback reflecting the students' mastery of the environment.
*   **Relatedness**: The agency structure binds students' fates together. To survive financial crises (e.g., paying the weekly rent), students must form tight bonds, negotiate, and collaborate internally and externally.

## 2. Experiential Learning (Kolb's Cycle)
RNP Manager heavily employs Kolb's Experiential Learning model:
1.  **Concrete Experience**: An agency goes bankrupt due to poor financial management.
2.  **Reflective Observation**: The agency must undergo an "Audit RH" to understand why they failed (reviewing the Event Log).
3.  **Abstract Conceptualization**: Developing a new financial strategy or restructuring the hierarchy.
4.  **Active Experimentation**: Returning to the market, recruiting new members, or merging with a wealthier agency to test the new strategy.

## 3. Gamification vs. Game-Based Learning
It is vital to distinguish RNP Manager from superficial gamification (often derisively termed "pointsification", i.e., just adding badges). 
RNP Manager leans closer to a **Simulation** or **Game-Based Learning Framework**. The entire ecosystem—including the UI, the notifications, the terminology (taxes, dividends, M&A)—is part of a cohesive narrative world. This "Vibe" creates a magic circle where taking risks is encouraged and failure is pedagogical rather than punitive.

## 4. The Peer Instruction and Social Constructivism paradigm
Vygotsky's theories on Social Constructivism are materialized in the "Peer Review" and "Mercato" modules. Students do not just learn from the platform or the teacher; they learn by evaluating the work of rival agencies and by negotiating their own value on the transfer market. This forces meta-cognition and critical analysis of their peers' strategies.
