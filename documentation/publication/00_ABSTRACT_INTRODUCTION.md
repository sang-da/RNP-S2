# 00 - Abstract & Introduction

## Abstract
Traditional project management courses often struggle with sustained student engagement and passive learning behaviors. "RNP Manager" (or "Studio Manager") is an open-source, gamified learning framework and Single Page Application (SPA) that transforms the classroom into a dynamic economic simulation. By casting students into "agencies" competing and collaborating in a simulated resource market, this system implements an "Event-First" architecture where actions, crises, and financial flows mirror real-world studio management. This paper details the pedagogical motivations, the gamification framework—including features such as a real-time 'Mercato', a central 'RNP Bank', and peer-review systems—and the Data-Driven React/Firebase architecture that makes it possible. We show how treating educational monitoring as a transparent "stock market" drastically increases intrinsic motivation, collaboration, and accountability.

## 1. Introduction
The transition from academic projects to professional agency environments requires more than just technical skill; it demands soft skills, strategic resource management, and emotional intelligence under pressure. However, standard educational platforms (LMS like Moodle, Canvas) are primarily document repositories and gradebooks. They track *completion*, not *behavior*.

RNP Manager was designed to bridge this gap. Rather than receiving intermittent grades, students manage a continuous flow of "Valeur d'Entreprise" (VE - Corporate Value) and "PiXi" (the in-game currency). 

The application serves two primary actors:
1. **The Administrator (Teacher/Jury)**: Acts as the "Game Master" and "The Market," capable of injecting macroeconomic crises, validating deliverables, and adjusting parameters in real-time.
2. **The Student**: Acts as a professional within an agency, managing bank accounts, hiring peers through the Mercato, initiating or surviving Black Ops (corporate sabotage), and delivering missions to stay solvent.

By releasing RNP Manager as an Open Science project on Zenodo and an open-source GitHub repository, we invite the academic and pedagogical engineering communities to study, adapt, and expand on this paradigm of active, gamified learning.
