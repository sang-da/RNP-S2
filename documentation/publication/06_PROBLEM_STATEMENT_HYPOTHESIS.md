# 06 - Problem Statement and Core Hypothesis

## 1. The Core Problem in Higher Education (The Teacher's Pain Points)
Before designing RNP Manager, we identified a set of recurring, systemic issues in traditional project-based learning, which we classify as the **"Siloed Academic Project Syndrome"**:

1.  **The Passenger Syndrome (Free-rider problem)**: In standard grouping, highly motivated students carry the workload while disengaged students passively consume the grade. Traditional peer-evaluation methods (typically a Google Form at the end of the year) are retrospective and often plagued by social pressure, failing to induce behavioral change *during* the project.
2.  **Lack of Real-World Consequence**: If a student fails a deadline in a standard class, they lose points. In the professional world, a missed deadline costs money, damages reputation, and can lead to firing. The academic environment lacks a safe mechanism to simulate these high-stakes economic and social stressors.
3.  **Administrative Overload**: Teachers spend exorbitant amounts of time grading iterative deliverables, managing interpersonal group conflicts, and adjusting grades. This leaves very little time for actual mentorship, coaching, or creative intervention.
4.  **Temporal Disconnect (The Black Box)**: Assessments are periodic (Mid-terms, Finals). Students operate in the dark, leading to a "cramming" mentality rather than sustained, continuous effort.

## 2. Our Objective and Purpose
Our primary objective was to build a system that intrinsically forces collaboration, strategic planning, and continuous accountability, all while offloading the administrative tracking to an algorithm. 
We wanted to transition the teacher from the role of a "Grader / Corrector" to a "Game Master / Mentor / Investor."

## 3. The Core Hypothesis
**H1:** By substituting standard academic grades (0-20) with a dual-currency simulated economy (Corporate Value / VE + Liquidity / PiXi) driven by real-time market mechanics, students will exhibit significantly higher sustained engagement and self-regulation.

**H2:** By introducing a fluid labor market ("The Mercato") where students can be fired, hired, or resign, the system will naturally self-correct the "Passenger Syndrome" through peer-to-peer accountability, drastically reducing teacher mediation.

**H3:** Utilizing a Data-Driven UI with an Event-Sourcing architecture (where the application itself is the single source of truth) will eliminate grading-related disputes between students and educators.
