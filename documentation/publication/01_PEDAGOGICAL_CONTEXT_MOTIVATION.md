# 01 - Pedagogical Context and Motivation

## 1. The Limits of Traditional Assessment
In higher education, specifically in project-based learning (design, software engineering, business), the classic assessment loop relies on the "Submission -> Grade" model. This traditional model suffers from several critical flaws:
*   **The Black Box Effect**: Students do not see how their grade fluctuates during the process, only at the end.
*   **Lack of Consequence**: A missed deadline simply lowers a grade, but it doesn't trigger "bankruptcy" or affect peer agencies, isolating the failure.
*   **Static Roles**: Students in a group often fall into static roles ("the worker," "the free-rider") with no systemic incentive to reorganize.

## 2. The Gamification Paradigm Shift
To counter these flaws, RNP Manager shifts the paradigm from a *class* to a *market economy*. 

### From Grades to "Valeur d'Entreprise" (VE)
Instead of grades out of 20 or 100, student groups (Agencies) are evaluated through their Corporate Value (VE). The VE acts like a stock price. It is public, transparent, and fluctuates based on actions, deliverables, and market events. 

### From Attendance to Economy (PiXi)
We introduced a virtual currency: PiXi. 
Students earn PiXi as a salary based on their agency's performance and their personal involvement. They must use PiXi to "buy" access to resources, pay "rent" for their agency, or invest in the "Mercato" to hire other students.

## 3. Active Learning via "Vibe Coding" and Simulation
The pedagogical intent is to create a "Vibe"—an immersive simulation that forces students into an active stance. If an agency runs out of PiXi, it faces bankruptcy and must be bailed out, merge with another agency, or dissolve. This introduces *risk*, a crucial element in adult learning that is notoriously difficult to simulate safely in an academic environment.

By making the rules explicit (Rule #1: The App is the Single Source of Truth) and transparent, students shift their focus from "pleasing the teacher" to "optimizing their agency's strategy in the market."
