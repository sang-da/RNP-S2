# 02 - Gamification Framework and Mechanics

This chapter details the specific game mechanics designed to foster engagement, peer collaboration, and strategic thinking.

## 1. The Economy: PiXi and RNP Bank
The financial system is the heartbeat of RNP Manager. Each student and each agency possesses a bank account.
*   **Weekly Rent**: Agencies must pay rent. If they lack funds, they trigger a financial crisis.
*   **Salaries**: Students receive weekly salaries. The amount can be influenced by internal agency voting (Democracy) or the agency's performance multiplier.
*   **Peer-to-Peer Transactions**: Students can transfer funds, enabling a true micro-economy (e.g., paying a student from another agency for freelance graphic design work).

## 2. The Mercato (HR and Recruitment)
Rather than locking teams for an entire semester, RNP Manager introduces the "Mercato" (Transfer Market).
*   **Unemployment**: Students can be fired or resign, entering the "Unemployed Pool."
*  **Recruitment**: Agencies can spend PiXi or VE to recruit top performers from the pool or poach them from other agencies.
*   **Team Dynamics**: This creates a realistic HR pressure. High-performing students become highly sought after, while agencies must maintain good internal conditions to prevent talent drain.

## 3. Missions and Deliverables
Instead of standard "homework," tasks are presented as Missions.
*   **Unlock Mechanics**: Some missions are locked behind prerequisites (e.g., reaching a certain VE or paying an unlock fee).
*   **Special Simulations**: "Black Ops" or "Crisis" scenarios can be triggered by the Admin to test agency resilience (e.g., a simulated server crash, a PR disaster).

## 4. Peer Reviews and Juries
To maintain fairness and reduce the administrative burden on the single teacher, RNP Manager relies heavily on Peer Reviews.
*   Students evaluate other agencies.
*   The system uses an algorithm to detect anomalous reviews (Review Audits) and normalizes the scores.
*   **Jury Mode**: External professionals can be given specific access to evaluate the final submissions, directly injecting funds or VE into the agencies they favor.

## 5. The "Black Market" and The Backdoor
To reward curiosity and technical hacking, the game includes hidden features. "The Backdoor" allows attentive students to bypass certain rules or gain hidden insights, rewarding out-of-the-box thinking.
