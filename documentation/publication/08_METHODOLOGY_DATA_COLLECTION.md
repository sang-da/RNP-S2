# 08 - Methodology and Data Collection

For the scientific publication, documenting *how* the success of the RNP Manager is evaluated is crucial. The platform's architecture allows for a multi-layered, mixed-methods research design.

## 1. Quantitative Data: Telemetry and The Event Log
The Firebase backend and the "Event-First" architecture provide an unprecedented amount of quantitative data without requiring manual tracking.
*   **Transaction Velocity**: Measuring the volume of peer-to-peer (P2P) transfers in PiXi to evaluate collaboration between rival agencies.
*   **Market Volatility (VE Fluctuations)**: Analyzing how rapidly agencies' Corporate Values change compared to traditional grade distributions (reducing the standard deviation over time).
*   **Platform Engagement**: Daily Active Users (DAU), average session length, and the frequency of logins outside of standard classroom hours (a strong proxy for intrinsic motivation).
*   **Audit Trail**: Analyzing the history of the "Mercato" (how many students were fired, traded, or resigned) to map the socio-dynamic instability of the cohort.

## 2. Qualitative Data: Surveys and Phenomenological Observation
Quantitative data explains *what* happened, qualitative data explains *why*.
*   **System Usability Scale (SUS)**: Measuring the UI/UX friction of the application to ensure the "Vibe Coding" approach has not obstructed usability.
*   **Intrinsic Motivation Inventory (IMI)**: Administered mid-semester and post-semester to assess the students' perceived autonomy, competence, and pressure.
*   **Semi-Structured Interviews**: Conducting interviews with both "Top Performers" (students who thrived in the Mercato) and "Bankrupt Users" (students who faced simulated failure) to document emotional resilience and stress management.

## 3. Comparative Cohort Study
To validate the hypotheses, the publication should ideally compare:
*   **Control Group**: A cohort undergoing the project with standard LMS (Moodle/Canvas) and traditional 0-20 grading.
*   **Experimental Group**: The cohort utilizing the RNP Manager framework.
*   **Variables compared**: Final deliverable quality (blind-graded by external juries), drop-out rates, reported stress levels, and peer-evaluation satisfaction scores.

## 4. The Role of the AI (Groq) in Evaluation
We evaluate the efficacy of the LLM integration by comparing the time teachers spend grading/assessing. 
Metrics include:
*   Time taken to issue weekly feedback.
*   The frequency of dynamic event creation (Crises/Black Ops) generated manually vs. generated via the "Creative Director" AI.
